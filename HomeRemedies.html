Apple Cider Switchel


A nutritious, invigorating, probiotic drink made with apple cider vinegar that reduces blood sugar, enhances healthy gut bacteria, lowers cholesterol, lowers insulin, helps loose weight, improves immunity, helps with digestion and consistency, and increases energy and vitality.

Ingredients

  • 4 slices ginger
  • 3 ¾ cups water, divided
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • juice of ½ –1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon honey

Directions

  • Place ginger in one cup of water in a small pot and bring to a boil. Let cool.
  • Pour the ginger water, remaining water, apple cider vinegar, juice from half a lemon, a pinch of salt and your choice of sweetener into to a quart mason jar. Stir and adjust lemon and sweetness to your taste.
  • Store in a pitcher or mason jar, either in the fridge or at room temp (if drinking throughout the day).

Ginger Lemon Honey Tea


A warm tea that contains honey, lemon and ginger which helps to increase immunity, detox, improve digestion, and has anti-inflammatory advantages.

Ingredients

  • 1 Inch piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • 2 Cups water
  • juice of 1 lemon
  • 1- 2 Tbsp Honey, or to taste

Directions

  • Cut the ginger into disks.
  • Bring the water and ginger to a boil. Cover, reduce heat to low and let steep for 5-10 minutes.
  • Remove ginger from water.
  • Add the lemon juice and honey to the water and stir to dissolve honey.
  • Serve tea in mugs.

Golden Milk


The ingredients used to make golden milk have antibacterial and antiviral properties, such as turmeric, which can protect your body against infections. Your immune system may also be strengthened by their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups almond, coconut or rice milk
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons grated fresh ginger
  • 1 inch fresh turmeric, peeled
  • 1 pinch freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon coconut oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1-2 tablespoons honey

Directions

  • Place all of the ingredients in a blender and puree until smooth. If your blender isn’t very powerful, pour through a mesh strainer.
  • Pour into a medium pot and simmer until warmed through, about 5 minutes.
  • Serve milk in mugs

Tulsi (Basil) Tea


Some respiratory diseases ranging from cold and cough to bronchitis and asthma are prevented by Tulsi tea. For the respiratory system, it has immunomodulatory (helps to improve immunity), antitussive (helps to relieve cough) and expectorant (helps to remove the phlegm) properties that provide relief.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh tulsi leaves (alternatives are 1/2 teaspoon of dried tulsi leaves or 1/3 teaspoon of tulsi powder)
  • Honey (optional)

Directions

  • Boil 1 cup of filtered water and pour it over 1 teaspoon of fresh tulsi leaves
  • Cover the water in a pot or mug and let it steep for 20 minutes (or longer, if you want to maximize the health benefits).
  • Strain the leaves, add honey if desired, and enjoy.

Rasam


Rasam is rich in thiamine, folic acid, vitamin A, vitamin C, niacin, and riboflavin, which are essential vitamins. Some of these vitamins, which keep the body healthy, also act as antioxidants. As a fairly good source of minerals, rasam is an ideal food to be included in a balanced diet for individuals.

Ingredients

  • 10 curry leaves
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 6 dry red chili
  • 4 green chilli
  • salt as required
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 litre water
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 8 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 handful coriander leaves
  • 2 teaspoon refined oil

Directions

  • Set aside a sprig of curry leaves, 2 dried red chillies, mustard seeds and grind all the rest of ingredients to a coarse paste.
  • Take a saucepan with water and add the ground paste to it. Put it over medium flame and mix well.
  • Add salt to taste, and leave it to boil.
  • Put a saucepan over a low flame and heat oil in it. When the oil is warm enough, add cumin seeds, crushed peppercorns, garlic, green chilli and remaining red chillies along with curry leaves.
  • Temper for a minute and then pour it over the ready rasam.
  • Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice

Homemade Yogurt


Yogurt can enhance the gut microbiota, be high in protein, calcium, vitamins and live culture, or probiotics. These can offer bone and teeth protection and help prevent digestive problems. On a diet for weight loss, low-fat yogurt can be a useful source of protein. The immune system may is also boosted by probiotics.

Ingredients

  • 42 ounces organic milk, (whole, 2% or skim milk)
  • 1 packet yogurt starter or 3 tablespoons of storebought yogurt

Directions

  • Pour the milk into a large glass, microwave-safe bowl.
  • Heat the milk in the microwave on high for 10 minutes. Using an instant read thermometer, check the temperature of the milk. Keep heating in 1 to 2 minute increments until the temperature has reached 180 degrees fahrenheit.
  • Remove the milk and let it cool to 112-115 degrees fahrenheit. This process can be sped up by using an ice water bath.
  • Pour 1 cup of the milk into a small glass. Sprinkle the packet of yogurt starter on top and thoroughly mix it in.
  • Pour the small glass of milk back into the large bowl and stir to combine.
  • Incubate using an oven. TUrn the light on for the oven and place the jars inside. Leave the yogurt for 6-8 hours based on your preference for the firmness and tartness of the yogurt.
  • Once the incubation is complete, remove the glass jars and refrigerate.
  • Before serving, you can add any toppings, such as fruit and granola.
