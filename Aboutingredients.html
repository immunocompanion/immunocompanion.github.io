Know What's Going in Your Body

Your immune system plays a very vital role in ensuring your body's protection from harmful substances. Without it, you would constantly get sick because of germs such as bacteria and viruses. Scroll down to learn more about the ingredients used in Immunocompanion's natural remedies.


Milk


Milk is a liquid food rich in nutrients provided by the mammaries of mammals. Milk contains nutrients that improve the immune system, such as probiotics, vitamin D and immunoglobulins, which in turn reduce the risk of allergies. Though bone-strengthening calcium is mainly linked to milk, it can help minimize cardiovascular problems and the risk of having a stroke.


Black Pepper


Black pepper is a flowering vine belonging to the Piperaceae family, grown for its fruit which is also known as perpercorn. It is usually dried and used as a spice and seasoning agent. But black pepper is more than just a spice for a table. It includes not only anti-inflammatory, antioxidant and antibacterial actions, but also benefits that improve the immune system.


Turmeric


Turmeric is a flowering plant of the Zingiberaceae ginger family, Curcuma longa, the roots of which are utilized in preparing food. By increasing the body's immunomodulating capacity, turmeric is a natural way to help strengthen the immune system.


Honey


Honey is a sweet, viscous food compound produced by honey bees and some insects associated with it. Bees produce honey through regurgitation, enzymatic activity, and water evaporation from the sugar secretions of plants or from secretions of other insects. The phytonutrients in honey, as well as their antibacterial and antifungal power, are responsible for its antioxidant properties. They're also believed to be the explanation as to why raw honey has shown benefits for immune-boosting and anticancer.


Tulsi (Holy Basil)


In the Lamiaceae family, Ocimum tenuiflorum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is an aromatic medicinal plant It is native to the Indian subcontinent and widespread throughout the Southeast Asian tropics as a cultivated plant. Vitamin C and zinc are rich in Tulsi. It thus serves as a natural booster of immunity and keeps infections at bay. It has enormous anti-bacterial, antiviral and anti-fungal properties that protect us from a number of infections.


Cinnamon


Cinnamon is a spice obtained from the inner bark of the genus Cinnamomum of several tree species. In a wide variety of cuisines, sweet and savory dishes, breakfast cereals, snack foods, tea and traditional foods, cinnamon is mainly used as an aromatic seasoning and flavoring additive. It is a rich source of antioxidants such as polyphenols and proanthocyanidins that give the immune system a boost is the aromatic spice. In addition, it has antiviral, antibacterial, and antifungal properties that help reduce the chances of seasonal infections and respiratory diseases.


Lemon


The lemon, Citrus limon, is a small evergreen tree species native to South Asia, mainly North East India, in the flowering plant family Rutaceae. Lemons are an excellent source of vitamin C, a natural antioxidant that has antibacterial and antiviral properties and improves the immune system.


Apple Cider Vinegar


Apple cider vinegar is a vinegar produced from fermented apple juice, or cider vinegar. There are different ways that apple cider vinegar can improve your immunity. There are beneficial bacteria and prebiotics in the fermented beverage that have a positive impact on the immune system. It is antibacterial in nature and helps to combat the havoc in the body caused by pathogens.
